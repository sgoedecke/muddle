When a message can't be processed — malformed payload, a bug in the handler — we retry it forever, in place, at the head of the partition. One poison message blocks every message behind it indefinitely. We need a dead-letter queue: after N failed attempts, move the message aside and keep the partition flowing.

This is the missing piece in the load-shedding story that #861 and #823 are also part of. It's also structurally identical to the errored-socket retention in #847 — in both cases we hold onto something we should have let go of.
