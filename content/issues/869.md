Our payment provider guarantees at-least-once delivery, and occasionally delivers the same `charge.succeeded` webhook twice. We process both, and the customer gets charged twice. This is the worst kind of bug — irreversible, user-visible, and about money.

We need idempotency keys on the charge path. This is the same "irreversible action" hazard as the non-reversible migration in #831. It may also share the double-flush root cause in #812 — if the handler isn't idempotent *and* the write path can duplicate, we get two independent ways to double-charge.
