Our upstream guarantees at-least-once delivery and occasionally sends the same event twice. We process both and apply the side effect twice. When the side effect is a charge, the customer is billed twice — irreversible and user-visible.

We need idempotency keys, checked transactionally before we act. This is the same irreversibility hazard as the non-reversible migration in #831, and if the check-then-act isn't atomic we've just recreated the write race in #812 at the database layer.
