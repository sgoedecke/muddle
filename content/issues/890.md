At `debug` log level we log full request objects, including the `Authorization` header and session cookies. These end up in our log aggregator, in backups, and in any support bundle a customer sends us. Anyone with log access effectively has every active session.

Scrubbing has to happen at the point of logging, not in the aggregator — by the time it reaches the aggregator it's already persisted. Note this compounds the auth bypass in #884: a leaked token plus a cross-tenant read is a full compromise.
