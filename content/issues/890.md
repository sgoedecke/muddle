At `debug` level we log full request objects, including `Authorization` headers and session cookies. These land in our aggregator, in backups, and in any support bundle a customer sends. Anyone with log access effectively has every active session.

Scrubbing has to happen at the point of logging, not in the aggregator. This compounds the guard bypass in #884 (leaked token + skipped check = compromise), and the verbose logging also dumps parsed rows with PII, overlapping the parser work in #819.
