RSS on the gateway climbs steadily and OOMs at around the 6-hour mark under normal traffic. Heap snapshots show a growing set of `Buffer` objects retained by the connection map. Connections that error out aren't being removed from the map.

I suspect this is downstream of the clobbered-flush bug in #812 — a failed flush leaves the buffer referenced. It may also be the same unbounded-growth pattern as #861, just on a different data structure.

@@ sam-oduya | Oct 26, 2024
The connection-map cleanup runs on a `close` event, but errored sockets sometimes emit `error` without a subsequent `close`. That's your leak. Same missing-cleanup shape as the poison-message retention in #911.

@@ maria-alvarez | Oct 28, 2024
Confirmed with a 6h soak test. Adding an `error`-handler that also evicts. I'll double-check it doesn't race with the flush path from #812 — otherwise we trade a leak for a data-loss bug.

@@ dmitri-k | Oct 30, 2024
Merged a mitigation (evict on `error`) but leaving this open until the underlying #812 flush ordering is fixed, since that's the true root cause.
