Postmortem for Tuesday's incident. When the downstream store returned elevated 503s, every client retried immediately with no backoff, which turned a 20% error rate into a full outage in about 90 seconds. Classic retry storm.

Action items I can see: (1) exponential backoff with jitter everywhere, (2) a retry budget per client, (3) circuit breakers. The unbounded-queue issue in #861 made this much worse because the backlog never drained. This also interacts with the cold-cache stampede in #877 — recovery was delayed because caches were cold *and* the retries were hammering them.
