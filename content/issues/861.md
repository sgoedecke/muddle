The in-memory work queue has no upper bound. When consumers fall behind — during a deploy, or a downstream slowdown — it grows until the process OOMs. There's no backpressure signal to producers at all.

This is the amplifier behind the incident in #823: without a bound, a transient slowdown becomes an outage. It's also structurally the same as the connection-map growth in #847 and the missing dead-letter handling in #911. I'd argue these should share a design.
