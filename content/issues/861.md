The in-memory work queue has no upper bound. When consumers fall behind — a deploy, a downstream slowdown — it grows until the process OOMs. There's no backpressure signal to producers at all.

This is the amplifier behind the incident in #823: a transient slowdown becomes an outage. Proposal: a bounded queue with `drop-oldest` plus a producer-side signal. Note `drop-oldest` is exactly what the flaky test in #840 asserts, so fixing this may settle that too.
