Every deploy wipes the in-process cache, and on restart every request is a cache miss, so they all hit the database at once. We get ~30 seconds of elevated latency and 5xx while the cache refills. It's predictable and it happens on every single deploy.

Mitigations: request coalescing (single-flight) on misses, cache warming before we take traffic, and staggered rollout. This made the incident in #823 much worse — recovery couldn't happen while caches were cold and retries were stampeding. It also interacts with the stale-read bug in #948: warming the cache incorrectly could serve stale rows.
